Day oneThe Adventure Begins

Kathmandu to Pokhara to Ghandruk (6,360 ft)

In November 2017, I (along with eleven other trek mates) trekked to Annapurna Base Camp, to and back for eight long days. I had been reading about Annapurna for few years ‚Äî¬†especially the Annapurna Circuit. But considering the multiple factors, time being the prime, I decided to a relatively shorter trek to ABC.

This website is an attempt to create a virtual artifact of the trek. It is supposed to serve as an extended sketch of my itinerary: A mapping of where we walked, and a log of places where we stayed.

In the end, it was a long walk. But a good long walk. And one that began as so many adventures in Nepal do: on propeller planes.

Much to my suprise, I was stunned when I reached Pokhara. A quaint little town is full of beautiful valley views and lakes. From Pokhara we drive till Lower Ghandruk and from there we hike for an hour or two to reach our first tea house.

Day TwoHello Annapurna

Ghandruk (6,360 ft) to Chomrung (7,120 ft)

After 8 hours and gazillions stair cases later, we made it to Chomrung(7120 ft). Today it felt like trekking is not an easy-peasy affair. However, for consolation, we got to see sunrise at Annapurna massif.

Day two began with the view of Machapuchare:

Through out the trail we saw houses. Mountain houses. Houses with views. They had certain persona. None of the structure tried to be pompous. If it had a facade, it served a purpose. Only essentials.

And then every once in a while, we got clear views of Annapurna massif. We could easily see where we're headed. At least here the goal was literally clear.

Day threeHello Rains

Chomrung (7,120 ft) to Dovan (8,460 ft)

All the tea houses in Chomrung have Annapurna views. You wake, you open the sliding door to your balcony and there it is. The Annapurna massif, covered in snow. Sometimes clouds. Sometimes clear (only if you're lucky).

We set out for Dovan. The trail goes through make shift bridges. Iron bridges. Mules on bridges.

When you've tea-houses at 7,000ft, you sort of start expecting views from any window. Or doors. Here's an example.

We reached Dovan, soaked in rain, at around 5pm. It was not an intense rain but it was long rain. We lost our rights to complain after we reached the little hamlet that had panaromic views in any direction we looked.

Day fourMachapuchare

Dovan (8,460) to Machapuchare Base Camp (12,135 ft)

To Machapuchare Base Camp it started with a gradual ascent. Few minutes into the walk and we were encountering steepest ascents so far. The terrain on this stretch is marked with small snow bridges that need to be crossed which can be scary at times.

Small flowering plants adorn the trail with their colours. Mostly white and reddish brown. The mountains on either side are rocky and gigantic. Small waterfalls flow from either side into Modi Khola. Apparently is also marked as an avalanche prone area.

We ended our 8 hour long walk at Machapuchare Base Camp. The end goal was just two hours away. But it didn't matter. A good sleep ‚Äî that's what it mattered.

Day fiveThe Final Destination

Machapuchare Base camp (12,135 ft) to Annapurna Base camp (13,550 ft)

The final push.

The beauty of long walks like this is that you get used to it after 3-4 days. The backpack starts feeling as a part of your body. Shoes get acclimatized to your feet. Views get breathtaking. After four days on trails, I found this walk as no different. To start with, we saw Annapurna South for the first time in it's entirety.

By now it had almost become a ritual to watch sunrise over the peaks. Especially the Annapurna South, Annapurna I and Machapuchare.

Day sixSo long Annapurna

ABC (13,550 ft) to Bamboo (7,675 ft)

The day of descents.

Getting to the top is optional. Getting down is mandatory. — Ed Viestur

We had forgotten how much pain we had endured for the last five days once we saw the peaks. In a way it was satisfying ‚Äî so much so ‚Äî that every step that we took felt worth it. With a heavy heart and relatively lighter backpack we bid farewell to Annapurna.

It was a pretty long walk. Longest of so far. Solid 16km in 8 hours.

Day sevenHot Spring Awaits

Bamboo (7,675 ft) to Jhinu (5,770 ft)

Hot spring day!

We were craving for a good bath. And a natural hot spring at Jhinu was something we were looking forward to from day one. As soon as we reached Jhinu, I along with few other team mates squeezed together in the hot spring. It felt absurd at first but our bodies liked it a lot.

Day eightThe Last Walk

Jhinu (5,770 ft) to Syauli Bazaar (3,740 ft) to Pokhara

There's a sadness to packing your pack a final time on a walk like this. On the morning of our eight day, we did just that, and then were off. Off to Sayauli Bazar where a bus was waiting for us for Pokhara.
